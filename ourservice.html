Newcomers Employment and Skills Strategy Project

This Program will run during summer and will involve getting newcomer students summer job placements. The objective of the Program is to provide each participant an opportunity of Canadian Work Experience that will lead either to a permanent / part-time position, or return to an educational program that will enhance their present and acquired skill sets to meet future career objectives. Participants are selected after an interview and screening by the Project Coordinator.

WINA Multicultural Day

This event will include a multicultural potluck supper, storytelling, cultural performances, presentation about different countries. WINA Job Fair

The WINA Job Fair will seek to bring together employers, qualified professionals, skilled workers and entry-level individuals and most importantly international newcomer students under one roof.

Well-Being and Mental Health Program by WINA

WINA in partnership with several campuses and community partners will use this program as an initiative aimed to encourage positive mental health to educate, reduce stigma, and promote conversation. It can be one day, or a couple of days depending on our budget. International newcomer students will be equipped with resources on how to support individual self-care and resiliency. It will comprise of physical activities that helps to manage stress and boost mental well-being and educate them about positive mental health, including what it means to thrive. We will Invite counsellors to attend the event and host information sessions about mental health and well-being. Newcomer Information Session by WINA This online Info Session will provide information regarding pre-arrival to Canada, transitioning to university, Counselling, recruitment / retention in Nova Scotia. This virtual event will be used to answer questions international students may have regarding healthcare, housing, opening a bank account, transportation, and working as they are in preparation towards moving to Halifax.

Empowering Resiliency by WINA

This program for international newcomer students will seek to targeting community awareness, developing self-protection strategies, developing healthy coping techniques and sustaining life balance, engaging in sports and recreational activities, and building a diverse toolkit for healthy decision making and future choices.

Social Connections Program by WINA

This program will comprise of 3 months of one-on-one mentorship, once per week for up to two hours Networking events held every 2 months, with guest speakers on topics of interest to international newcomer students. Field trips and outings to community organizations, festivals or cultural events We will incorporate mentors many of whom are past immigrants, know what difficulties international newcomer students face here in Canada. We will use this opportunity to discuss Canadian life and culture, try new activities and hobbies, explore your local community and make new friends.

Settlement Support Program by WINA

This program will seek to provide international newcomer students with one-on-one settlement support, information referrals regarding visa status inquiries, housing, healthcare access, community leadership opportunities, fun social activities, pathways to permanent residency workshops, and other valuable information sessions. Immigration and visa advice will be provided by immigration experts as well as Inspirational immigrant speakers. We will offer job search support, online job application guidance, resume specialization support, cover-letter guidance, job interview preparation, and seminars led by guest speakers from various fields of work. It will also comprise of general information sharing about resources and services available in Halifax for international newcomer students.

WINA’s Anti-Racism, Diversity & Inclusion Project

WINA’s Anti-Racism, Diversity & Inclusion Project is closely aligned with the Community Support, Multiculturalism and Anti-Racism Initiative Program’s mission through its focus on promoting intercultural or interfaith understanding, as well as celebrations of a community’s history and culture, civic memory and pride or respect for a healthy democracy. Also in line with the mandate of Canada’s anti-racism strategy to combat racism and discrimination, WINA Anti-Racism, Diversity & Inclusion Project.

EmpowerLadies Program by WINA

This proposed program is for female international newcomer students to become part of their community through volunteerism, while helping them develop skills that will empower them to become an ambassador of change in their new schools. It provides free training in project management, leadership, employability, entrepreneurship, grants, and life skills, as well as assistance in the co-creation of service projects responding to identified community needs.

Employment Preparation Workshop (Online)

This workshop helps newcomers find employment by using innovative techniques to tap into the hidden job market and master their job search. In this workshop, we will provide international newcomer students with the right insight into Canadian workplace culture, strategic networking techniques, hidden job market, employer expectations, occupation-specific research and leveraging social media for your job search. They will also learn an overall understanding of numerous methods which they can use to successful job search in Nova Scotia.

Welcoming International Newcomers Program

This program will give our students the opportunity to: Participate in free supervised group activities. Meet and work with peer mentors and professional mentors. Connect to their community and learn about Canada / Nova Scotia culture. FREE field trips. Participate in sports, arts and recreational programs. Experience Nova Scotia (Halifax). Get help in finding part-time jobs. Participate in your community and gain volunteer experience.
